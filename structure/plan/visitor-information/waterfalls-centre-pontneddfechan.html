    The Waterfalls Centre, Pontneddfechan

     


    The Waterfalls Centre will be closing permanently from 4.00pm on Sunday 5 June. 
    After that date you can continue to access information at Cwm Porth and at the National Park Visitor Centre

    Address
    Waterfalls Centre,
    Pontneathvaughan Road,
    Pontneddfechan,
    Nr Glynneath,
    SA11 5NR


     

    Waterfall Countryis an enchanting part of the Brecon Beacons National Park and of Fforest Fawr Geopark, and the Waterfalls Centre in the village of Pontneddfechan provides a great starting point for any exploration of this beautiful area.

    The Centre houses an exciting interactive exhibition telling the story of Fforest Fawr Geopark and its many wonders, the formation of the waterfalls and the part that rocks have played in the lives of people of this area through history to the present time.
     
    It's an ideal place to start a walk along one of the many paths to the waterfalls and to visit the intriguing remains of the  gunpowder works and silica mines which hint at the hive of activity and industry that once filled this now tranquil place.

    One experience never to be forgotten is the walk behind the curtain of falling water at Sgwd yr Eira (literally the 'fall of snow')! Derek Brockway's  Weatherman Walking TV series also features a walk in this area.


    The Centre is staffed by extremely knowledgeable Information Officers, who will be pleased to give you all the advice you need to enjoy this spectacular part of the National Park. Whilst there you can purchase those all important maps and guides, including the exclusive Waterfalls Country Pack.  Also in stock are many other essential items for your walk to the waterfalls, as well as a variety of gifts and souvenirs to remind you of your visit.

    The Centre houses the popular Geopark exhibition that tells the geological story of the Brecon Beacons over hundreds of millions of years.  Take the time to explore the interactive facilities, and watch Ivor Coleman's Geopark film that brings the prehistory of the area to life.

     

    Services and Features

    • Information Officers available to give advice and directions
    • Shop stocking maps, guides and other essential items
    • Range of locally produced gifts and crafts
    • Informative Geopark exhibition area.
    • Hire of Waterfalls activity rucksacks
    • Snacks and drinks for your walk.
    • Public toilets opposite the Centre
    • Audio Trails available

     

    When To Visit Us

    Our opening hours are from 10.00am to 4.00pm  but the Centre will be closing permanently
    from 4.00pm on 5 June 2016

    We open Weekends Only from November until March

    The centre closes for dinner for half hour 

    Please telephone or call in for any information you require.
