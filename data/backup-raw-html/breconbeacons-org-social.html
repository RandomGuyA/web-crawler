    • Brecon Beacons National Park 16th Mar

      We have teamed up with our Ecologist to create a great new feature - Brecon Beacons Species of the Month – seasonal wildlife to look out for! MARCH - The Lesser Celandine (Ficaria verna) Harbinger of spring, treatment for a certain delicate complaint and William Wordsworth’s favourite plant…. Our native woodland wildflowers put on a fantastic display in early Spring. The Lesser Celandine is one of the earliest to flower and, despite its Welsh name of Llygad Ebrill (‘April’s Eye’), can appear as early as February. Its striking, golden yellow flowers (together with its glossy heart shaped leaves) often blanket the ground in woodlands, hedge-banks, damp fields and on the banks of streams and rivers. It’s a resilient little plant, with an ability to open and close its petals according to the weather – a feat noted by Wordsworth in one of 3 poems celebrating the little plant. “There is a Flower, the Lesser Celandine, That shrinks, like many more, from cold and rain; And, at the first moment that the sun may shine, Bright as the sun itself, ‘tis out again!” (From To the Small Celandine, William Wordsworth) And like many of our native wildflowers it has traditional herbal uses. Also known as pilewort, it was once believed to be a remedy for haemorrhoids because of the knobbly-shaped tubers attached to the roots! Gardens can be a fantastic place for nurturing wildflowers and March is a great month to begin – for lots of inspiration on what to plant and where, check out Plantlife’s The Wildflower Garden web pages http://www.plantlife.org.uk/wildflower_garden
